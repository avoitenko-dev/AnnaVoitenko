My City

Kyiv

Kyiv is Ukraine’s capital and largest city, one of the most popular tourist destinations in the country. This ancient city served as the capital of the powerful Kyivan Rus’ in the 9th century, a medieval state that laid the foundations for modern-day Ukraine, Belarus, and Russia. Currently, it is the city of opportunities, where tradition combines with modernity. It boasts a modern tech industry with a developed start-up scene and a spirited national culture that celebrates both Ukrainian traditions and international flavors. The hospitality of its people creates a sense of community that welcomes newcomers from all parts of the world and fosters a sense of belonging to its culture.

Interesting facts about Kyiv

  • Kyiv has a population of about 3 million (7th-most populous city in Europe) and occupies an area of more than 840 square kilometres (about 3 times bigger than Birmingham).
  • Arsenalna metro station is the deepest station in the world (over 105 m). This is a London-type station with no central lobby.
  • In May, a lot of chestnuts bloom in Kyiv. That is why Kyiv is also unofficially called the `chestnut` capital.
